about me!

hi! i'm jasmine, a high school student whos passionate about web design and AI! I made this website for Girls Who Code's 2023 Humanize AI Challenge. I really wanted to make this website about AI in social media because of how much I'm impacted by it. As a teenager who uses apps like Instagram and Youtube every day, I wanted to learn more about AI and teach others about it too!

  • Social Media Algorithms: A 2023 Guide for Every Network by HootSuite
  • The Impact Of AI & How It Is Used In Social Media by Statusbrew
  • What Is a Machine Learning Engineer? by Brainstation
  • Fei-Fei Li's Stanford Profile
  • Inside the good, bad, and very ugly of social media algorithms by Fastcompany
  • How filter bubbles isolate you by GCFGlobal

1. Describe a challenge you faced. How did you overcome it? How did you reach out for help?

One challenge was actually creating the website. I had a lot of ideas, but actually implementing them was hard. I also tried my best to make the website responsive to different screen sizes. This is a topic I'm passionate about and I want anyone to be able to see the website. I reached out for help by asking my friends with bigger monitors or tablets to take a look at the website and give me feedback. I also used a lot of online resources to help me out whenever I was stuck!

2. What did you learn from this process that will impact the way you interact with technology in the future?

Through coding this website, I will definitely be more appreciative of website design in general. It's harder than it looks! Additionally, after doing more research on social media addiction and echo chambers, I realized how I'm also impacted by these negative effects. I plan on being more careful with my information sources as well as enforcing my screen time better.

3. Why did you choose this format for your project?

I chose this format for the project because I wanted to work on my website-making skills. Last summer I attended SIP and learned the basics of web design. I loved the course, but I don't find myself using what I learned often. When I saw the challenge, I knew I had to make a website. I love designing and coding, so to me, making a website seemed like the only path!
